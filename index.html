Discover 3 must-see destinations in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples dotting the city's mountains and coastline to beaches with crystal clear waters. This seaside town has plenty of things to do year-round -- families can Spending time in a beachfront aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can Enjoy long walks to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist shrines that dot Busan's coastline and mountains have stunning architecture that will delight the photographers.

1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the far northeast of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea built on the seafront – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other. 

    Good for:
    ・ Story

2. Haedong Beomeo-sa

Beomeo-sa Temple is one of the largest shrines in South Korea. It is located high on the eastern edge of the mountain Geumjeongsa and is far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of the architecture of the Joseon Dynasty.

    Good for:
    ・ Story

3. Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in central Busan, is home to some of the city's most important monuments. You can see spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park has 2 museums – check out the traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and more than 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

Good for:
  • Couples
  • Families
  • Budget
